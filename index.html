Why a hard ICMR⁻ floor exists: with an NMOS input pair, the lowest usable common-mode input is ICMR⁻ = VTN + Vov12 + Vov5. The VTN term (0.54 V) is irreducible — you cannot get below it no matter how wide the devices.
Real silicon adds ~30–60 mV more: body effect on M1/M2 (VSB≈Vov5) raises VT by ΔVT=K1·(√(2φF+VSB)−√(2φF)) ≈ 40 mV, plus short-channel BSIM corrections — which is why the simulated wall sits near 0.85–0.9 V.
